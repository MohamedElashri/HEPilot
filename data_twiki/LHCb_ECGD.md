ECGD \-- Mentoring at LHCb =========================

touch with more experienced colleagues. (This is complementary to the
guidance they get from their supervisor or group leader.) Here is a
table of volunteer mentors \-- you can contact them by email or in
person, or through the ECGD team (see below).

If you\'re here to volunteer as a mentor, please go ahead and add
yourself to the table (or update/remove the line if your situation
changes). If you have a contact/info page at your home institute, you
can make your name double as a link to it.

Here \"country\" means those countries/areas about which the mentor can
offer guidance, and \"position\" is the type of post held by the mentor:
PostDoc, PhD student, Master Student, Engineer, Technician, Senior.

To \*\*contact a mentor\*\*, just write an email to her/him. If, for any
reason, you don\'t want to contact directly a mentor, you can write an
email to one of us (\[oyangur@ific.uv.es\](mailto:oyangur@ific.uv.es))
and we\'ll do it for you. (We can even find a mentor for you if you\'re
not sure who to approach).

More information on mentoring =============================

During the first \[ECGD meeting\![\](./LHCbECGD \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://indico.cern.ch/event/353338/),
1st December 2014, the topic of mentoring has been discussed. Following
the responses collected during the meeting and with the agreement of
LHCb management, it has been decided to establish a mentoring system at
LHCb. This of course heavily relies on participants, those who volunteer
to provide mentoring, and those who use them. Mentoring can be a very
helpful tool for coping with the intricacies of your job and your
career, and should also be fun and rewarding experience for all
involved.

The mentoring role can take several forms. Full mentoring would imply
regular meetings/discussions with the individuals or the group
associated with that mentor. But more limited interactions can also be
very useful, such as advice on job applications / CVs (especially when
someone is applying for a position in a country whose system they\'re
not familiar with).

Resources =========

\* ECFA and EPS-HEPP have prepared a \[short
report\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/pub/LHCb/LHCbECGD/ecfa-291_ECFA-HEP-evaluation.pdf)
about how recruitment committees should evaluate candidates from
experimental HEP. While it\'s aimed at non-HEP committee members, it
does a good job of explaining how things work (or should work) in an HEP
selection process, and what the recruitment committee pays attention to.
So if you\'re applying for a post, worth a read. There\'s also a \[cover
letter\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/pub/LHCb/LHCbECGD/Evaluation-accompanying_letter-final-general.pdf).
