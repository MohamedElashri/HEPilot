General information ===================

The Real Time Analysis (RTA) project develops and maintains the
real-time processing of LHCb\'s data for Run 3 and beyond. The project
organigram is shown above, while the work packages and current project
responsibles are listed in the table on the right. For a description of
the terms used in the organigram, jump to
\[this\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/RealTimeAnalysis#Organigram_glossary)
section of the page.

\*\*The RTA project meets weekly at 14:30 CERN time on Friday
afternoons.\*\* Please see the relevant INDICO
\[category\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://indico.cern.ch/category/10878/)
for the CERN room numbers and VIDYO connection details of the meeting
you are interested in. Individual work packages hold their own meetings,
which you can find in the same INDICO category, with more details given
on the individual work package TWiki pages (see table on the right).

\*\*Getting involved:\*\* the RTA project welcomes contributions from
all members of LHCb! Please read this TWiki, study the work package
TWikis to understand what existing work is ongoing in the area where you
wish to contribute, and get in touch with the relevant WP coordinator(s)
or project management. Institutes which wish to join the RTA project in
a more structured way should in the first instance discuss with the IB
chair.

\*\*Project culture:\*\* members and contributors are asked to take note
of the \[LHCb code of conduct\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](http://lhcb.web.cern.ch/lhcb/lhcb_page/collaboration/organization/LHCb-Code-of-Conduct.pdf)
as well as the following, and to respect it

\* The \[contributor\'s covenant\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/)

\* Use of gender neutral language is strongly encouraged

\* Those with more stable jobs are expected to take the more
bureaucratic jobs on their shoulders and defend the interests of those
with less stable jobs

\* Tasks assigned to non-tenured(-track) staff should directly benefit
their intellectual and/or career development

\* We welcome feedback. Feedback should be presented in a polite manner
which helps to develop a solution or contains a constructive suggestion

\*\*Mailing lists:\*\* see the archives of the \[RTA project mailing
list\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://groups.cern.ch/group/LHCb-RTA-general/default.aspx)
and the more general LHCb upgrade computing mailing list
\[LHCb-UpgradeComputing-Discussion\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://groups.cern.ch/group/LHCb-UpgradeComputing-Discussion/default.aspx).
The RTA work packages have their own mailing lists, please look under
their Twikis. We also have a mailing list \[for the review of RTA
technical papers\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://groups.cern.ch/group/lhcb-rta-rd-paper-review/default.aspx).

\*\*Conferences:\*\* A list of RTA related conference talks and posters
is maintained \[here\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://gitlab.cern.ch/lhcb-rta/conference-talks/-/wikis/home).
RTA members wishing to submit conference abstracts should get in touch
with the project leader and/or deputies, in order to ensure that our
submissions are coherent both within the RTA project and across other
related LHCb projects. The approval of the plots within the RTA group
follows the procedure described in \[twiki
page\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/RealTimeAnalysisPlots).
The \[operations
twiki\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCbInternal/OperationPlotApproval)
and the \[note\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://indico.cern.ch/event/775967/contributions/3225437/subcontributions/267894/attachments/1766188/2867866/OperationPlotsProposal.pdf)
on approving operations plots also contain useful information for
conference participants. Conference proceedings play an important role
in documenting work done in the project, and all RTA conference speakers
are expected to write proceedings to accompany their talks.

\*\*Acknowledgements for conference proceedings:\*\* Please follow the
guidance given for short-author-list publications
\[here\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/RealTimeAnalysisPaperReviews).

\*\*Documentation:\*\* In addition to conference proceedings, all
project members are encouraged to document their work in
short-author-list publications whenever possible. Furthermore, project
deliverables are only considered completed once they are documented. The
appropriate documentation will vary from case to case, however in
general RTA members should aim to document each deliverable with both an
internal LHCb note containing all the relevant technical and
implementation details, and a short-author-list publication advertising
the importance of this work to a wider audience. A list of RTA-related
notes and publications can be found
\[here\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/RealTimeAnalysisDocumentation).
The \[LHCbDocs\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/LHCbDocs)
Twiki page contains useful information when preparing notes or papers.

\*\*RTA paper reviews:\*\* Guidance for proponents and reviewers of RTA
short-author-list publications can be found
\[here\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/RealTimeAnalysisPaperReviews).

Planning ========

Project planning is primarily carried out by defining deliverables and
tracking their progress. The deliverables for each work package will
eventually be listed on the relevant work package\'s TWiki page, in the
meantime the old USPG activities
\[page\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/LHCbComputingUpgradeUSPGPlanning)
provides a useful snapshot of ongoing upgrade software activities
relevant to RTA. The RTA project reports regularly to the relevant
stakeholders:

\*\*IB meetings:\*\* these occur roughly once per month, see the
relevant INDICO \[category\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://indico.cern.ch/category/10878/)
for the dates.

\*\*LHCC reviews:\*\* see the \[LHCb calendar\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_
LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](http://lhcb.web.cern.ch/lhcb/images/Calendar/Calendar_2020.html)
for the relevant dates.

\*\*RRB reports:\*\* these are scheduled for the weeks of April 15 and
October 28 2019.

\*\*Collaboration weeks:\*\* see the \[LHCb
calendar\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](http://lhcb.web.cern.ch/lhcb/images/Calendar/Calendar_2024.html)
for the relevant dates. A parallel session is held on the Monday of each
LHCb week, and a project status report given to the collaboration in the
relevant time slot later in the week. Additional talks may be requested
in case there are significant developments in specific areas of the
project which deserve to be brought to the wider attention of the
collaboration.

\*\*A&S weeks, computing workshops, and hackathons:\*\* see the \[LHCb
calendar\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](http://lhcb.web.cern.ch/lhcb/images/Calendar/Calendar_2024.html)
for the relevant dates. General RTA reports are not expected at these
weeks, but specific presentations on topics of particular interest may
be requested. All RTA members are strongly encouraged to participate in
the hackathons and to attend the external computing workshop each year.

Producing Simulated samples for RTA studies
===========================================

If you wish to produce simulated samples for upgrade studies, then so
long as your request is below 100k events there is no formal approval
procedure. Open an issue in
\[https://gitlab.cern.ch/lhcb-rta/mc-requests/-/issues\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis
\_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://gitlab.cern.ch/lhcb-rta/mc-requests/-/issues)
filling the information:

\* Who will receive notifications \* Event types \* Number of events \*
Year: 2022, expected 2023, 2024, expected 2025 \* Output format: DIGI,
XDIGI, DST

Once this is filled, assign RTA liaison (fterzuoli) and Project Leader
(Montana) and the request will be processed.

(X)DIGI samples do not contain any reconstruction and are most useful
for HLT1, reconstruction or HLT2 studies. A list of previously produced
MC samples (not complete) is available in
\[https://its.cern.ch/jira/browse/LHCBGAUSS-1837\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis
\_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://its.cern.ch/jira/browse/LHCBGAUSS-1837)

Applying for RTA conference talks =================================

Most RTA conference talks concern one specific area of work in the
project, and are proposed with a specific speaker already in mind.
However there are also general RTA overview talks which are important
for publicising the project outside LHCb and for which the speaker is
not defined in advance. General talks at \"physics\" conferences are
advertised by the Speaker\'s Bureau and you should check their \[page of
available talks\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://lhcb-sb.web.cern.ch/lhcb-sb/sb_advertised.php)
and apply in the usual way. General talks at technical conferences are
usually coordinated with other LHCb projects and advertised on the RTA
general mailing list. To apply for them please email the project leader
giving a brief motivation and indicating that you have financial support
from your group leader for the trip. If multiple applications are
received priority will be given to colleagues who have not yet had a
chance to present an RTA talk.

Organigram glossary ===================

\*\*Institutional Board\*\*: the body which is responsible for ensuring
that the project is sufficiently staffed to fulfil its objectives.
Institutional board members represent institutes, or groups of
institutes, which have made an ongoing commitment to work within the RTA
project. The institutional board oversees the progress of the project,
advises the project management, and elects the project leader.

\*\*Project coordination\*\*: consists of the project leader, their
deputies, and the coordinators of the project\'s work packages. Its
primary responsibility is to oversee the work packages and ensure the
optimal assignment of people to tasks/deliverables in order to meet the
project\'s objectives. If the participating institutes are unable to
adequately staff certain areas of the project, project coordination
takes a proactive role in recruiting volunteer developers from across
the collaboration to ensure the project\'s success. The IB chair is
invited, ex officio, to attend project coordination meetings. Decisions
are taken by consensus wherever possible.

\*\*Work packages\*\*: each work package groups together a set of
related \*\*deliverables\*\*. Work package coordinators are responsible
for defining the deliverables, ensuring that they are completed and
documented on time, and prioritising effort where necessary to achieve
the project\'s objectives.

\*\*Deliverables\*\*: a deliverable is a specific, self-contained, part
of the project. It roughly maps to a \"category\" in
\[GitLab\](https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/view/LHCb/GitLab) \[project
organisation terms\![\](./RealTimeAnalysis \_ LHCb \_
TWiki_files/external-link.gif)\](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/verify/#categories).
Examples include an algorithm; a related group of algorithms; a hardware
accelerator (or part thereof); a specific data, simulation, or software
quality report. Every deliverable has an assigned \*\*responsible
developer\*\* who commits to ensuring the timely completion of the work.
The developer is also responsible for integrating volunteer
contributions relevant for their deliverable. A deliverable can only be
considered completed if it has been adequately documented, as judged by
the work package responsible.

\*\*Working group and voluntary developers\*\*: the RTA project
encourages contributions from all members of LHCb, even if they (or
their institute) are unable to make a commitment to a specific
deliverable or deliverables. Examples of this are physics working group
members who need to implement a specific trigger selection for their
particular analysis, or the development of a new tool or method which
